1. Hubble Space Telescope

The Hubble Space Telescope is a space telescope that was launched into low Earth orbit in 1990 and remains in operation. It has greatly contributed to our understanding of the universe.

2. James Webb Space Telescope

The James Webb Space Telescope is a large, space-based observatory set to launch in 2021. It will be used to study every phase in the history of our Universe, from the Big Bang to the formation of solar systems capable of supporting life on planets like Earth.

3. Chandra X-ray Observatory

The Chandra X-ray Observatory is a space telescope launched by NASA in 1999. It observes X-rays from high-energy regions of the universe, such as the remnants of exploded stars.

4. Kepler Space Telescope

The Kepler Space Telescope was a space observatory launched by NASA in 2009 to discover Earth-sized exoplanets.

It discovered thousands of exoplanets before its mission ended in 2018.

5. Spitzer Space Telescope

The Spitzer Space Telescope is an infrared space telescope launched by NASA in 2003.

It observes the universe in infrared wavelengths, studying objects such as dust clouds, stars, and galaxies.
