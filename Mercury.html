Mariner 10 (1974-1975)

Mission Objectives: Mariner 10 was the first spacecraft to visit Mercury. Its primary objectives were to map the surface, study Mercury's atmosphere and magnetic field, and characterize its geological features.

Key Findings:

  • Mariner 10 provided the first close-up images of Mercury, revealing a heavily cratered surface similar to the Moon's.
  • It discovered Mercury's thin atmosphere, composed mainly of helium, with traces of hydrogen, oxygen, sodium, and potassium.
  • Mariner 10 also confirmed the presence of a weak magnetic field around Mercury, suggesting the existence of an iron-rich core.

MESSENGER (2004-2015)

Mission Objectives: The MErcury Surface, Space ENvironment, GEochemistry, and Ranging (MESSENGER) mission aimed to orbit and extensively study Mercury to understand its composition, magnetic field, geology, and history.

Key Findings:

  • MESSENGER discovered water ice and organic molecules in permanently shadowed regions near Mercury's poles.
  • It provided detailed maps of Mercury's surface, revealing diverse geological features such as volcanic plains, cliffs, and crater formations.
  • The mission confirmed the presence of a global magnetic field and a partially liquid outer core, challenging previous theories about Mercury's internal structure.
  • MESSENGER also investigated Mercury's exosphere, the thin atmosphere surrounding the planet, and studied its interaction with the solar wind.
